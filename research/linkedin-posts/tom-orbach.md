## Post 2: The Visual Rhythm & "Squint Test" Formatting Engine
- **Date Collected:** 2026-06-19
- **Source Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tomorbach_i-know-it-sounds-stupid-but-if-you-want-activity-7436676402122387456-jXom?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADsvczIBwk31Kfp-odj_kyZwsSYXsHjRRIY

### Original Content
> I know it sounds "stupid" but if you want to go viral on LinkedIn...
>
> Make your post look like this:
>
> Short lines.
>
> Then a longer thought that explains what you mean, gives context, and keeps people reading because it feels natural (and not like AI wrote it).
>
> Then short again.
>
> See what just happened? Your eyes just glided through it.
>
> Here's my trick:
>
> I've published 500+ LinkedIn posts. Every time, I squint my eyes until I can barely read the words. If all the "blocks" look the same size, I rewrite. If they vary like a rhythm, I know people will actually stop and read.
> 
> I only hit "Post" when it's beautiful.
>
> Try it: zoom out on this post right now (CMD + minus). Long, medium, short, long, short, medium, short, long, medium, long, one word.
>
> Lovely.

### AI-Native Tactical Insight
- **Core Pivot:** Upgrading copywriting distribution from raw text output to a highly optimized typographical layout that exploits visual pacing to reduce cognitive friction and maximize dwell time.
- **B2B SaaS Application:** To lift organic engagement rates and prevent the 100Hires distribution engine from looking automated or sterile, the content team must enforce Orbach's visual rhythm protocol before publishing any asset:
  1. **De-biasing AI Formatting Textures:** Legacy AI copywriters often output uniform blocks of text or rigid bulleted lists that readers instantly filter out as "AI sludge." The growth editor must manually refactor every text body, structuring the post into a typographical wave: a single-sentence hook, a short contextual line, a medium-length narrative block, and an isolated one-word emphasis. This layout mimics human thought progression and hooks mobile scrollers.
  2. **The Pre-Publishing "Squint Test" Checklist:** Enforce a hard quality assurance (QA) step in the content pipeline. Before clicking post, the creator must zoom out (`CMD + minus`) or squint until the text characters blur into pure geometric shapes. If the layout shows a uniform brick pattern, the post must be rejected and rewritten. If the layout reveals a dynamic, jagged asymmetrical pattern, it clears the check—ensuring maximum scrolling friction reduction and higher platform retention.

### AI-Native Tactical Insight
- **Core Pivot:** Weaponizing reverse psychology (reactance theory) and sharp pattern interrupts in performance marketing to bypass buyers' defensive gates and accelerate autonomous software evaluation.

## Post 5: The Wiz Growth Manifesto (Pattern Interruption & Un-gated Shareability)
- **Date Collected:** 2026-06-19
- **Source Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tomorbach_what-3-years-at-wiz-taught-me-about-b2b-marketing-activity-7374335119655944193-UUQk?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADsvczIBwk31Kfp-odj_kyZwsSYXsHjRRIY

### Original Content
> What 3 years at Wiz taught me about B2B marketing ✨
>
> I'm the Director of Growth Marketing at the fastest-growing security company in history. 
>
> Here's what actually worked so far:
>
> 1. Kill your boring content 
>
> Create viral "drops" instead - unexpected stunts that surprise people. Like a toy store for CISOs or Kubernetes training with puppies. Most fail. But the wins build your brand.
>
> 2. Use the "pattern interruption" formula 
>
> Take something universally familiar and give it ONE weird twist. Customer case study → over cocktails at a bar. Musical → about cybersecurity. But you must be crystal clear and easy to understand. Don't change everything (confusing) or nothing (boring).
>
> 3. Stop gating everything 
>
> We stopped thinking about leads and started thinking about shares. Create content so good people want to share it, not hide it behind forms. Leads came regardless
>
> 4. Make customers look good 
>
> Send certificates when they hit milestones. Put their name bigger than your logo. When customers look brilliant online, they share it with their network.
>
> 5. Different isn't risky
>
> Looking exactly like everyone else is risky. Everyone in cyber uses dark/red visuals and fear-based messaging. We used bright colors and humor. CISOs loved us for giving them 5 minutes of not hating their job.
>
> —
>
> 🎁 Full playbook with examples in my latest free newsletter >> https://lnkd.in/d9u8ZBgB

### AI-Native Tactical Insight
- **Core Pivot:** Overhauling enterprise distribution from defensive, fear-based messaging and gated lead-capture to an aggressive, un-gated pattern interruption model engineered for high-velocity organic shareability and customer ego-validation.
- **B2B SaaS Application:** To capture attention in saturated enterprise environments, the growth architecture for a platform like 100Hires must abandon defensive corporate theater and deploy Wiz’s high-signal distribution plays:
  1. **The Share-First Un-gating Protocol:** Eliminate the friction of legacy lead-generation forms for top-tier content. Instead of gating a high-value technical sourcing framework behind an intrusive form, release it completely open and optimize it exclusively for peer-to-peer shareability. High-intent pipeline flow occurs naturally when the asset is high enough quality to be cross-posted natively within internal recruiting and operations Slack channels.
  2. **Corporate De-risking via Brand Deflection:** In crowded tech spaces where competitors rely on clinical, sterile branding, execute deliberate pattern interrupts. Refactor core content assets—such as a case study tracking enterprise hiring velocity—by applying a single unexpected, humanized layer (e.g., using sharp humor, bright visual interfaces, or interactive gamified elements). Standing out structurally removes marketing friction and positions the platform as the obvious, modern alternative in a sea of identical legacy solutions.
